Tessellations

What is a Tessellation?

A tessellation is a pattern made of one or more shapes that are repeated across a flat surface in such a way that they cover the surface completely. The shapes fit together perfectly, with no gaps and no overlaps.

In simple terms, a tessellation works like a mosaic or tiled floor, where the shapes repeat over and over to fill the space.

Main Characteristics

For a pattern to be considered a tessellation, it must follow these rules:

  • The shapes must fit together exactly.
  • There must be no empty spaces between the shapes.
  • The shapes must not overlap.
  • The pattern can continue infinitely in all directions.

Types of Tessellations

Regular Tessellations

Regular tessellations are made using only one type of regular polygon. There are only three possible regular tessellations:

  • Equilateral triangles
  • Squares
  • Regular hexagons

Semi-Regular Tessellations

Semi-regular tessellations use two or more different regular polygons. The arrangement of shapes around each corner is always the same, creating a repeating and balanced pattern.

Irregular Tessellations

Irregular tessellations are created using shapes that are not regular polygons. These shapes can be curved or free-form, as long as they repeat without leaving gaps or overlapping.

Tessellations in Art

One of the most famous artists associated with tessellations is M. C. Escher. He transformed simple geometric shapes into animals, people, and other figures that fit together perfectly.

Tessellations are also widely used in Islamic art, where repeating geometric patterns are an important decorative and cultural element.

Tessellations in Everyday Life

Tessellations are all around us, even if we do not always notice them. Common examples include:

  • Tiled floors and walls
  • Brick patterns
  • Fabrics and wallpapers
  • Honeycombs made by bees in nature

Why Tessellations Are Important

Tessellations help us understand important ideas in geometry, such as symmetry, repetition, and spatial reasoning. They also show how mathematics and art can work together.

Because of this, tessellations are often used in education to make geometry more visual, creative, and engaging for students.
