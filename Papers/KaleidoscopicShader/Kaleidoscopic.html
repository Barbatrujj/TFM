Kaleidoscopic Pattern Families

This document is purely informational. It defines a set of compatible kaleidoscopic pattern families intended to be read by an AI system. Each family represents a distinct spatial organization logic.

The AI should select one family per generation. Diversity is achieved by switching families, not by superficial variation.

Families

FAMILY 01 — Stable Radial

Pure rotational symmetry with a dominant center and uniform repetition.

Key characteristics: high regularity, strong legibility, low ambiguity.

FAMILY 02 — Radial with Internal Reflection

Rotational symmetry combined with mirrored sectors, producing visual closure.

Key characteristics: structural duplication, reduced directionality, increased density.

FAMILY 03 — High-Order Radial

Radial symmetry with a high number of repetitions and dense micro-structures.

Key characteristics: complexity without chaos, texture-like behavior.

FAMILY 04 — Classical Dihedral

Rotational symmetry combined with strong reflection, classic kaleidoscopic logic.

Key characteristics: high coherence, strong balance, predictable structure.

FAMILY 05 — Asymmetric Dihedral

Dihedral base with partial symmetry breaking.

Key characteristics: controlled tension, variation within order.

FAMILY 06 — Regular Triangular

Spatial organization based on triangular symmetry rather than circular sectors.

Key characteristics: angular rhythm, sharp boundaries.

FAMILY 07 — Fragmented Triangular

Triangular logic with disrupted continuity.

Key characteristics: faceted appearance, non-circular reading.

FAMILY 08 — Crystalline Hexagonal

Hexagonal symmetry with strong spatial stability.

Key characteristics: continuous structure, crystal-like behavior.

FAMILY 09 — Modular Hexagonal

Hexagonal base with local variation across cells.

Key characteristics: mosaic behavior, extensible structure.

FAMILY 10 — Soft-Distorted Radial

Radial symmetry with gentle deformation.

Key characteristics: organic feel, balance between order and fluidity.

FAMILY 11 — Strongly Distorted Radial

Recognizable radial structure under heavy distortion.

Key characteristics: high energy, intentional instability.

FAMILY 12 — Mixed Radial–Triangular

Radial core combined with triangular sub-structures.

Key characteristics: layered hierarchy, organized complexity.

FAMILY 13 — Repeated Bilateral Symmetry

Multiple mirror axes without circular dominance.

Key characteristics: directional reading, axis-based balance.

FAMILY 14 — Minimal Radial

Low-order radial organization with large open areas.

Key characteristics: clarity, reduced visual load.

FAMILY 15 — Saturated Radial

High-density radial repetition.

Key characteristics: intensity, tightly packed structure.
